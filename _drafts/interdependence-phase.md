> In class today, Moe mentioned asking other students to help revise/edit papers. I twitched.. and told him and the class to be careful doing that.

I tend to do this; I stress NOT assisting each other on homework and assignments, but never really introduce alternatives or train them how to support each other approrpiately.

> I shouldn't have panicked. Nelson 1991 explains this "interdependence" is a welcome intermediate step between dependence on the instructor and independence in writing. I may have inadvertently stifled that progression. I should make a point of it next class.

I didn't make a point of it. I should do a little more #research though. For example, does making them work on their own necessarily prevent their progress? Does every student eventually come out of that stage, or do some do as many teachers, includng myself, suspect, which is that they are always going to be dependent on someone else.

> Dependence is refering to the way students depend on the teacher or tutor or other authority figure to identify or correct grammar mistakes or relying on grades for motivation.
