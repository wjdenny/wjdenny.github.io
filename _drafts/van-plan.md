My experience working abroad changed me. One of the things that I lost was my tolerance for doing things that I don't want to do. For awhile now, I've always had trouble with the idea of paying rent for space I don't need just to be able to live a "normal" life.

The motto of this mid-life crisis is that I only live once, and I'm not making any sacrifices for people I don't like, respect, know, or who otherwise need my time and attention. This pretty much rules out any well-to-do company bosses or university presidents, landlords, or marketing execs that profit off our uncritical consumption of goods produced with cheap labor and no concern for the environment.

So! I'm going to live in a van. I'm going to plan it carefully, and take baby steps as I earn the money to do these things.

Writing up this plan accomplishes a few other things. It helps me design it, of course. It also helps concerned members of my tribe to see that I'm putting thought into it and will, eventually, know what I'm doing. And maybe it can help me practice my writing skills and habits and be useful to other people considering living in a van.

The mission here is not just to avoid paying rent. Though, there is that.
* reduce expenses 
  * to catch up on savings
  * to spend more on the things, people, and causes that matter to me
* reduce my impact
  * the van is designed to be as minimal and zero waste as I can tolerate
  * to teach through example; no one needs to buy cases of half-liter plastic water bottles. stop it.

On to the plans!

## Kitchen
I learned to be fairly comfortable cooking with nothing but hot water and a thermos, and got good at using a microwave to the point of it feeling like a luxury. I plan on using a single-burner propane stove to hit the marks on minimal and zero waste. The tanks can be refilled and used again and again as long as the tank is undamaged; and when it is, it can be recycled. I only need one burner, most often to heat water, but I could occasionally use a pan. 

The big gap I am seeing here is baking. Potatoes would be an excellent way to stay fed and healthy, and I love a baked potato. Yes, there are propane ovens made for small spaces, but it seems like a lot of waste heat. I'll have to look into the technicals of a propane oven before I really decide, but maybe it won't be so bad if I just dice up potatoes and fry them up.

## Shower
Not neccesary! A gym membership can be had for less than $40/month to allow me 24/7 access to thousands of gyms across the country. They have showers, and it gets me out of the van and into a gym on a daily basis.

For days where I can't do this, for any reason, there are [natural zero waste wet wipes](https://wildminimalist.com/blogs/news/3-ingredient-diy-zero-waste-baby-wipes) that I can easily make myself and they'll do in a pinch. I'm talking like when I'm out camping or too sick to get out of bed.

## Bathroom
Most of the time I will be outside of the van, but there does need to be a plan in place for convenience or emergency. I'm looking into building a simple composting toilet

## Build stages
I don't feel comfortable building it all at once. So likely, I would start in during spring months to do the framing and insulation. I want to have the plan in place and just tackle pieces as I have a free weekend.

## A/C and heating
I'll go without any cooling. A good exhaust fan and air flow will be enough, along with hanging out in coffee shops or work during hot times, which I usually do anyway.

I will have some kind of heating. I've seen some builds that have a little wood stove, which is nice but more hassle than I want to deal with. I saw one that had hired someone to put in a heater that somehow pulled a bit of gas off the tank of the van and used that to heat the interior. That would likely be my first choice. Might be a bit expensive at first, but it seems worth it.
