Kirkwood Community College's Adjunct Faculty Advisory Committee hosted the annual Iowa Conference on Higher Education, held Saturday, October 24th. With several presentation tracks, the theme was “T3: Techniques for Instruction, Current Topics in Education and Technology in the Classroom.” Held at the Linn Regional Center and open to all educators. The conference is known for keynotes with timely messages, engaging presenters, opportunities for networking with other educators across the state, and a terrific venue.

The presentation was the result of a semester of using the [GoReact] service in an EAP presentations class and months of discussion with GoReact sales and technical support. Presented with Kirkwood Community College's [TechScouts] team in a series of education technology presentations and demonstrations titled "Edu-Tech for Today and Tomorrow."

You may view the [slides] from this presentation on this site. The description in the conference program was as follows:

>  The TechScout Committee is a vanguard unit of Kirkwood’s Learning Technology Advisory Committee (LTAC) that explores ways various cutting-edge technologies can be integrated into our teaching. In this session, the Scouts will demonstrate a few edu-tech tools that we use or are currently experimenting with, but we also want to provide a forum for your questions and recommendations. This session requires 2 hours.  First hour will be devoted to introducing participant to new technology. Second hour will allow participants to play with the new technology.
