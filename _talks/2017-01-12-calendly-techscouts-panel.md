The way we were doing placement interviews for incoming students who needed English testing as taking up a lot of valuable staff time. ELA Advising staff would have to coordinate between individual students and indivudal raters to agree on a time to conduct the interview. At best, this required two emails. I researched an app solution to save ELA advising staff time when trying to coordinate incoming ELA students with qualified KDOSEOPI raters. The result was Calendly, a minimal scheduling app that takes the back-and-forth out of making appointments.

As a member of "TechScouts" at Kirkwood Community College, I gave a brief presentation on the potential use of the scheduling webapp Calendly to a group of faculty. The [deck] I put together shared the basic information, but most of the presentation was a demo of how easy it was to set up and for students to use when scheduling time with faculty. It was nice to share my enthusiasm for a product that I had been using in my work for quite awhile.
